STEP 7 - En Passant (special pawn capture rule)
  En passant is a special pawn capture that can only happen immediately after
  an opponent pawn moves two squares forward from its starting row.
  The capturing pawn must be on the 5th rank (row 5 for white, row 4 for black).
  The capture lands on the square the opponent pawn passed through, and the
  opponent pawn is removed even though the capture did not land on its square.

  To implement this:
  - Add a field to track the "en passant target square" (the square a pawn
    just skipped over). Set it to null by default.
  - In makeMove(), after a pawn moves two squares, set the en passant target
    square to the square it skipped over.
  - After any other move, reset the en passant target square to null.
  - In PawnMC (or in validMoves logic), add the en passant capture as a
    candidate move when the en passant target square is set and a pawn is
    in the right position.
  - In makeMove(), when an en passant capture is made, remove the captured
    pawn from the board (it is NOT on the end square of the move, it is
    one square behind it).

STEP 8 - Castling (king and rook special move)
  Castling is a special move where the king moves two squares toward a rook
  and the rook jumps to the other side of the king. There are two types:
  kingside (short) and queenside (long).

  Conditions that must ALL be true for castling to be legal:
  - Neither the king nor the rook involved has moved yet this game
  - There are no pieces between the king and the rook
  - The king is not currently in check
  - The king does not pass through a square that is under attack
  - The king does not end up in check after castling

  To implement this:
  - Add boolean fields to track whether each king and each rook has moved
    (e.g. whiteKingMoved, whiteKingsideRookMoved, whiteQueensideRookMoved,
    blackKingMoved, blackKingsideRookMoved, blackQueensideRookMoved)
  - In makeMove(), set the appropriate flag to true when a king or rook moves
  - Add castling as candidate moves in validMoves() when all conditions are met
  - In makeMove(), when a castling move is detected (king moves two squares),
    also move the rook to its new square on the same move
